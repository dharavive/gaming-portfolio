MEMORY GAME

  1. To start the game, click on the first image to reveal it, and then click on the second image to see if it matches.
  2. If the images match, they will stay revealed, and you can move on to the next pair.
  3. If they don't match, they will be hidden again, and you will need to remember their positions for the next turn.
  4. The game will continue until you have matched all the images. The fewer turns you take to match all the images, the higher your score will be.

CHESS GAME

  1. The objective of chess is to checkmate the opponent's king, which means trapping it so that it cannot escape capture.
  2. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.
  3. Each piece has its own unique way of moving, and players must use them strategically to capture their opponent's pieces and protect their own.
  4. In chess, each piece has a point value: pawns are worth 1 point, knights and bishops are worth 3 points, rooks are worth 5 points, queens are worth 9 points, and the king is priceless.

HANGMAN GAME

  1. The objective of the game is to guess the hidden word or phrase by suggesting letters. For each incorrect guess, a part of the hangman is drawn. The game ends when the hangman is complete or the word or phrase is guessed correctly.
  2. Players can only guess one letter at a time, and they can't guess the same letter twice.
  3. The game continues until the word or phrase is correctly guessed or the hangman is complete.
  4. To make the game more challenging, players can agree on a theme for the words or phrases, such as fruits, animals, or countries.
